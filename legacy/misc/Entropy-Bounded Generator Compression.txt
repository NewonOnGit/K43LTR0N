dim(H_R) = d_R < infinity

Locality prevents global mixing from destroying local structural distinctions at arbitrarily small timescales.

## Section 2 — Local Operator Algebra and Generator Dimension

## 2.1 Local Hilbert Space Structure

Let R be a bounded spatial region.

By Assumption A1:

dim(H_R) = d_R < infinity

B(H_R)

This is isomorphic to the full matrix algebra:

M_{d_R}(C)

Therefore:

dim(B(H_R)) = d_R^2

This is the maximal number of linearly independent operators acting locally on region R.

---

## Lemma 2.1 — Finite Operator Basis

For any bounded region R, the space of linear operators acting on H_R admits a finite basis of size d_R^2.

**Proof Sketch:**

Choose an orthonormal basis { |i> } for H_R, with i = 1 to d_R.

Define matrix units:

E_{ij} = |i><j|

There are d_R^2 such operators.

These span B(H_R).

Therefore, B(H_R) is finite-dimensional.

QED.

---

## 2.2 Local Hamiltonian Generators

Let the global Hamiltonian H be decomposable into local or quasi-local terms:

H = sum over regions R of H_R + interactions

Each H_R is an element of B(H_R).

Define a local generator refinement as:

A new linearly independent operator term added to H_R.

Formally:

A refinement at region R is an operator X in B(H_R) such that:

X is linearly independent of previously included generator terms.

---

## Definition 2.1 — Independent Generator Refinement

Let G_R be the set of generator terms currently included in region R.

An operator X in B(H_R) is an independent refinement if:

X is not in the span of G_R.

---

## Theorem 2.1 — Bounded Independent Local Refinement

Under Assumption A1, the number of independent generator refinements per bounded region R is bounded above by d_R^2.

**Proof:**

By Lemma 2.1, B(H_R) has dimension d_R^2.

Therefore, any linearly independent set of operators in B(H_R) has size at most d_R^2.

Hence, the number of independent generator refinements is finite and bounded by d_R^2.

QED.

## Section 3 — Meta-Closure and Compressive Constraint Recursion

This section formalizes the behavior of constraint recursion inside a finite-dimensional local operator algebra.

We show that meta-closure at the generator level is necessarily compressive under the entropy and locality constraints established in Sections 1 and 2.

---

## 3.1 Constraint Operators

Let R be a bounded region with Hilbert space H_R of dimension d_R.

Let B(H_R) denote the local operator algebra.

Define a **constraint** on region R as a linear condition on allowable states or generators.

Formally:

A constraint C is represented by a subspace V_C ⊆ B(H_R).

Equivalently, C specifies a linear restriction on admissible operators.

Example:

- Symmetry constraint
- Conservation law
- Projection restriction
- Generator inclusion/exclusion rule

Constraints are therefore elements of the lattice of subspaces of B(H_R).

---

## Definition 3.1 — Constraint Refinement

Let C_0 be an initial constraint subspace V_0 ⊆ B(H_R).

A refinement step produces a new constraint C_1 with subspace V_1 such that:

V_1 ⊆ V_0

and V_1 ≠ V_0.

A refinement is **independent** if:

dim(V_1) < dim(V_0).

That is, it strictly reduces the admissible operator space.

---

## 3.2 Meta-Closure Operator

Define the meta-closure operator M acting on constraints:

M: V → M(V)

where M(V) encodes constraints that arise from modeling the system under constraint V.

Meta-closure requires:

If a constraint V is internally represented within the system, then the modeling of that representation must also satisfy admissibility under V.

Formally:

M(V) ⊆ V

A constraint is **meta-closed** if:

M(V) = V

---

## Theorem 3.1 — Finite Constraint Descent

Under Assumption A1 (finite local operator dimension), any strictly descending chain of independent constraint refinements in region R is finite.

**Proof:**

Let dim(B(H_R)) = N = d_R^2.

A refinement corresponds to selecting a proper subspace of B(H_R).

Each strict independent refinement reduces dimension by at least one.

Thus:

dim(V_0) > dim(V_1) > dim(V_2) > ...

Since dimension is a nonnegative integer bounded above by N, this chain must terminate after at most N steps.

Therefore, no infinite strictly descending chain of independent local constraint refinements exists.

QED.

---

## 3.3 Compressive Meta-Closure

Meta-closure recursion corresponds to iterating M:

V_0
V_1 = M(V_0)
V_2 = M(V_1)
...

If each step yields a strict refinement:

dim(V_{n+1}) < dim(V_n)

Then by Theorem 3.1, this process terminates in finite steps.

If at some stage:

M(V_k) = V_k

Then V_k is meta-closed.

Thus:

Meta-closure converges locally in finitely many steps.

---

## Corollary 3.1 — Local Meta-Closure Stabilization

For any bounded region R, generator-level constraint recursion stabilizes after finitely many independent refinement steps.

Infinite meta-escalation at generator level is impossible without violating finite operator dimension.

---

## 3.4 Entropy Interpretation

Since:

dim(B(H_R)) = exp(2 S_max(R))

The maximal length of an independent refinement chain is bounded by:

exp(2 S_max(R))

Thus:

Entropy density bounds the maximal depth of independent generator refinement.

Constraint refinements propagate only through local interactions.

Therefore:

Even globally, infinite refinement would require:

dim(H_U) > dim(H_K)

A bijective linear map requires equal dimensions.

Therefore no isomorphism exists.

QED.
