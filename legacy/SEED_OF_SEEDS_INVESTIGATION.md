## CORE IDENTIFICATION

### The Seed

R = self-relating difference. Not merely an object or operation but the minimal primitive that distinguishes, relates, and can act on its own output. Four primitive modes under self-action:

| Mode | What happens | Framework realization |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| Stable coincidence | R returns to itself | Identity, fixed point, R(R)=R |
| Stable opposition | R returns as opposite | Inversion, polarity, N²=−I |
| Cancellation | Distinction fails to survive | Null, zero, kernel |
| Recursive propagation | R generates further distinctions | Growth, structure, bridge chain |

### The Unification Thesis

The framework's two co-primitives — P.1 (recursive substrate) and P.2 (productive distinction) — are the two halves of R:

- P.1 = the self-relating half (persistence, repeatability, re-entry)
- P.2 = the difference half (sustained differentiation between states)

R is not a third primitive. R is what P.1 and P.2 are when viewed as a single entity rather than a co-primitive pair. The co-primitive formulation breaks R into its irreducible components for rigorous derivation; the Seed of Seeds formulation recognizes that these components are never found apart.
