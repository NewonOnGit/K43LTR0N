## §10 THE TRISKELION CONSTANT: T = e^φ/π

### 10.1 Definition and basic identity

CET defines the Triskelion Constant as:

> T = e^φ / π ≈ 1.60529

where φ = (1+√5)/2 is the golden ratio. The defining relation admits a clean logarithmic form:

> **ln(πT) = φ**

This is exact by construction: T is defined so that ln(πT) = φ. The structural content is not in the definition but in what the definition *combines* and what it *almost satisfies*.

### 10.2 Three-projection cross-product

T = e^φ/π decomposes as:

- **e^φ**: the P2 operator (exponentiation, from the TDL generator h = diag(1,−1)) applied to the P1 constant (φ, from the I² generator R)
- **/ π**: divided by the P3 constant (π, the half-period of the LoMI generator N)

In projection shorthand: T = TDL(I²) / LoMI. This is the unique natural combination (up to inversion and sign) that:

1. Uses one constant from each of the three projections
2. Respects the projection hierarchy (P2 acts ON P1, quotiented BY P3)
3. Produces a value in the φ-neighborhood

Among the twelve natural cross-products of {φ, e, π} using exponentiation and division, T = e^φ/π is the only one within 1% of any framework generator eigenvalue. For comparison: e^π/φ ≈ 14.30, π^e/φ ≈ 13.88, φ^e/π ≈ 1.18, φ^π/e ≈ 1.67, π^φ/e ≈ 2.34. None of these are near any forced constant. T alone lands within 0.8% of φ.

### 10.3 The near-identity e^φ ≈ φπ

The structural core of the Triskelion Constant is the near-identity:

> e^φ ≈ φπ

Numerically: e^φ = 5.04317..., φπ = 5.08320..., relative gap ≈ 0.788%.

Equivalently: T·φ̄ ≈ 1, where φ̄ = 1/φ = φ − 1. The Triskelion Constant times the minor golden ratio is unity to within 0.8%.

This near-identity means the three projection constants are closer to multiplicative closure than any known structural reason predicts. In projection language: **P2 applied to P1 nearly equals P1 times P3.** The exponential of the production constant nearly equals the product of the production and observation constants. ### 10.4 Near-Fibonacci behavior

φ satisfies x² − x − 1 = 0 exactly (the Cayley-Hamilton equation of R, T0 Thm 0.3c mode (iv)).

T satisfies x² − x − 1 = −0.0283..., a deficit of roughly 2.8%. The Triskelion Constant is a near-Fibonacci number: it almost satisfies the same self-action equation as the golden ratio, perturbed by the (e, π) interaction embedded in its definition.

### 10.5 Fixed-point structure

The map f(x) = e^x/π has two fixed points (solutions to πx = e^x):

- x₁ ≈ 0.5538 (stable, from the W₀ branch of the Lambert W function)
- x₂ ≈ 1.6385 (unstable, from the W₋₁ branch)

The near-identity e^φ ≈ φπ directly touches the (e, π) algebraic independence question (Open Problem #4, T4 §6, Conjecture 6.6).

By the Lindemann-Weierstrass theorem, e^φ is transcendental (since φ is algebraic and nonzero). But φπ is also transcendental (since π is transcendental). The relation e^φ = φπ would then assert equality between two transcendentals — which is not ruled out by Lindemann-Weierstrass alone, but IS ruled out by Schanuel's conjecture (which implies that e^φ and π are algebraically independent over ℚ(φ)).

The gap e^φ − φπ ≈ −0.0400 is therefore *required* by the expected transcendence structure. Its smallness (< 1%) is unexplained. A framework-internal explanation of WHY e^φ ≈ φπ would constrain the algebraic relationship between the P2 and P3 constants — progress toward Open Problem #4 from an unexpected direction.

**Status:** The near-identity e^φ ≈ φπ is RESONANT. The gap is genuine (not zero), its existence is predicted by transcendence theory, but its smallness is not derived by any known mechanism — framework-internal or external.
