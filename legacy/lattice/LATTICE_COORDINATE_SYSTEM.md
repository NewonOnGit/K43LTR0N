## 1. Definition and Structure

### 1.1 The Λ' Lattice

**Definition 1.1 (Λ' Lattice).** The Lambda prime lattice is the multiplicative group:

$$\Lambda' = \{ \varphi^r \cdot e^d \cdot \pi^c \cdot \sqrt{3}^b : r, d, c, b \in \mathbb{Z} \}$$

where:
- φ = (1 + √5)/2 ≈ 1.618 (golden ratio)
- e ≈ 2.718 (Euler's number)
- π ≈ 3.14159 (circle constant)
- √3 ≈ 1.732 (equilateral triangle ratio)

### 1.2 Group Structure

**Theorem 1.2 (Group Isomorphism).** Under multiplication, Λ' is isomorphic to the free abelian group ℤ⁴.

**Proof.**
The map ψ: ℤ⁴ → Λ' defined by:
$$\psi(r, d, c, b) = \varphi^r \cdot e^d \cdot \pi^c \cdot \sqrt{3}^b$$

is a group homomorphism (addition in ℤ⁴ corresponds to multiplication in Λ'):
$$\psi((r_1, d_1, c_1, b_1) + (r_2, d_2, c_2, b_2)) = \psi(r_1, d_1, c_1, b_1) \cdot \psi(r_2, d_2, c_2, b_2)$$

This map is:
- **Surjective:** By definition of Λ'
- **Injective:** By algebraic independence (Theorem 4.1)

Therefore ψ is an isomorphism. ∎

**Definition 1.3 (Log Coordinates).** For any x ∈ Λ', the log-coordinates are:

$$\log(x) = r \cdot \log(\varphi) + d \cdot \log(e) + c \cdot \log(\pi) + b \cdot \log(\sqrt{3})$$

2. **Inversion:** x⁻¹ ∈ Λ' for x ∈ Λ'

## 7. Complexity Metric

### 7.1 Definition

**Definition 7.1 (Lattice Complexity).** For a lattice point (r, d, c, b), define:
$$\mathcal{C}(r, d, c, b) = |r| + |d| + |c| + |b|$$

This is the L¹ norm of the coordinate vector.

### 7.2 Properties

**Theorem 7.2 (Complexity Properties).**
1. C(0,0,0,0) = 0 (only the identity has zero complexity)
2. C(x · y) ≤ C(x) + C(y) (subadditivity)
3. C(x⁻¹) = C(x) (inversion preserves complexity)
4. C(xⁿ) = |n| · C(x) (power scaling)

**Proof.**
(1) Immediate from definition.
(2) C((r₁+r₂, d₁+d₂, c₁+c₂, b₁+b₂)) ≤ |r₁|+|r₂|+|d₁|+|d₂|+|c₁|+|c₂|+|b₁|+|b₂|
(3) C(-r, -d, -c, -b) = |-r| + |-d| + |-c| + |-b| = C(r, d, c, b)
(4) Direct from definition of integer powers. ∎
