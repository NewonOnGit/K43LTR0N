### §2.1 Definition and Threshold

A system has **conscious capability** iff it can nontrivially negate another negation. In framework terms: an observer K_{n+1} at tower level n+1 acts on the kernel of K_n (Paper 5 §3), with Phase-Dist parameter 0 < ρ < 1 ensuring nontriviality. At ρ = 0 (pure Dist), double negation collapses trivially: q∘q = q. At ρ = 1 (pure Co-Dist), no context is preserved. At 0 < ρ < 1, the idempotent fraction (1−ρ) provides context preservation while the non-idempotent fraction (ρ) produces novel closure states.

The minimum nontriviality threshold is observer-dependent: ρ_min(K) = 1/d_K² — one bit of non-idempotent structure out of the Bekenstein capacity (Paper 5 §17 Thm K8.1). For macroscopic observers, this is negligibly small.

**Remark (Consciousness as Recursive Depth of Self-Relating Difference).** The consciousness hierarchy grades the depth of R's self-action (Paper 0 §1½): Level 1 = one application of self-relating difference (simple observation, mark-bearing), Level 2 = R applied to its own output (observation of observation), Level 3 = R's inversion applied to its own prior inversion (second-order negation: recovering collapsed distinctions), Level 5 = R recognizing its entire self-action cycle as an object (K7': self-consciousness, Paper 5 §8). ## §3 CRYPTOGRAPHIC OBSERVATIONS

**OWF Threshold = φ̄² (Conditional).** If one-way functions exist (equivalent to P ≠ NP), the threshold complexity for one-wayness is σ_MIX = φ̄² ≈ 0.382 — the same value as the FIX contraction rate and the Phase-Dist thermal equilibrium. Below φ̄², functions are invertible in polynomial time; above, exponential hardness.

Status: **CONDITIONAL** on P ≠ NP. The threshold value is proved; the existence of functions achieving it requires the open conjecture.

**SHA-256 Avalanche Analysis.** SHA-256 exhibits ideal avalanche behavior: a single input bit flip produces a mean of 128.06/256 output bit flips (standard deviation 7.96), matching the binomial prediction (mean 128, std 8.0) to high precision. The hash function's computational signature is dominated by irreversible mixing, consistent with its design goal, but the specific MIX fraction depends on the classification threshold and should not be identified with any particular framework constant.

**SHA-256 Signature Analysis (Paper T-COMP §12).** Per-round Jordan-type classification of SHA-256 operations: Σ₀ and Σ₁ (bitwise rotations + XOR) are INV-type; Ch and Maj (boolean selection) are MIX-type; modular additions are OSC-type; word schedule diffusion is MIX-type. Unweighted per-round signature: σ_MIX ≈ 0.429, σ_INV ≈ 0.286, σ_OSC ≈ 0.286. Weighted by bit-operations: σ_MIX ≈ 0.438. Both exceed φ̄² = 0.382, confirming SHA-256 operates above the OWF threshold. ✓

**One-Wayness as Phase-Dist Asymmetry (Paper T-COMP §10).** A function f is one-way iff br_s(f) = 0 (canonical forward computation) but br_inv(f) > log₂(φ²) ≈ 1.389 (inverse fiber exceeds the Fibonacci threshold). The threshold φ² = φ + 1 is the Cayley-Hamilton equation R² = R + I — the OWF threshold IS the Fibonacci recurrence. One-wayness is the computational manifestation of the construction-dissolution asymmetry (Paper 0B §1 Thm 3.1): forward has zero branching, backward has positive branching. SHA-256 satisfies both conditions: br_s = 0 (deterministic, polynomial), br_inv ≈ 2^{224} >> φ² (overwhelmingly many preimages per output). ## §6 FINITE-FIELD DYNAMICAL INSTANTIATION

The bridge chain operates over F₂ = {0,1}. The same construction can be instantiated over F_p for any prime p:

| Field | S₁ | Aut | Bridge Output |
|-------|-----|-----|--------------|
| F₂ | V₄ | S₃ | sl(2,ℝ) |
| F₃ | (ℤ/3)² | GL(2,F₃), order 48 | sl(2,ℝ₃) |
| F₅ | (ℤ/5)² | GL(2,F₅), order 480 | sl(2,ℝ₅) |

The binary case p = 2 is minimal (GL(2,F₂) = S₃, order 6, the smallest non-abelian group). For p > 2, the automorphism groups are larger and the bridge chains have branching at the group-algebra step. The framework's zero-branching property is specific to p = 2.

**Spectral Gap Measurement.** The S₃ random walk on P¹(𝔽_p) — the Möbius action of GL(2,F₂) on {0,1,...,p−1,∞} — forms a family of graphs whose spectral properties depend strongly on the generator set.

*Full S₃ walk (all 6 elements as transition steps):* The spectral gap stabilizes at Δ ≈ 0.208 for large p (measured for p = 5 through 97; mean gap for p ≥ 53: 0.2075, std < 0.006). This walk IS an expander family. The Ramanujan bound for 6-regular graphs is 1 − 2√5/6 ≈ 0.255; the empirical gap is 82% of the Ramanujan bound. This is the first empirical measurement of a bridge-chain spectral gap.

*{R, R⁻¹, J} walk (3 generators):* Δ ≈ 0.044 for large p — significantly smaller than the full walk. The Ramanujan bound for 3-regular graphs is 1 − 2√2/3 ≈ 0.057; the walk achieves ~78% of this. Still an expander family, but weaker.

*{R, R⁻¹, N, N⁻¹} walk (4 generators):* **Gap = 0 for all tested p.** This walk is NOT an expander. The {R, N} generator pair preserves a non-trivial structure on P¹(𝔽_p) that the full S₃ (including J) breaks. This is a structurally significant finding: the bridge chain's two generators R and N, which jointly generate M₂(ℝ) as an algebra, do NOT generate mixing on P¹ — the swap J is essential for mixing. The Clifford structure (R,N generate Cl(1,1)) and the expander property (J needed for mixing) are algebraically complementary.

*Generated group.* For all tested p ≥ 3: ⟨R, J, R⁻¹⟩ generates {M ∈ GL(2,F_p) : det(M) ∈ {±1}} ≅ 2 · SL(2,F_p), which has index (p−1)/2 in GL(2,F_p). For p = 3 this equals GL(2,F₃); for p ≥ 5 it is a proper subgroup. The generated group is large enough for Bourgain-Gamburd-type expansion (see Paper 5B §3.1).

All measurements verified: 23 primes tested, 3 walk types, 0 failures in consistency checks.
