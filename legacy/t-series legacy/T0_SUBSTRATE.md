### §0 RELATIVE ORIGIN

The framework does not begin with a bare binary seed. It begins with the existence of a relative origin.

**Definition 0.0a (Framework).** A framework is a triple F = (L, C, Π) where L is a language structure, C is a set of constraints, and Π is an admissibility filter.

**Definition 0.0b (Closure Deficit).** The closure deficit of a description D within framework F is the functional δ(D|F) = α·Err(D;C) + β·Comp(D) + ρ·Viol(D;C,Π) with nonnegative weights, measuring the total cost of D's failure to achieve self-consistent closure within F.

**Definition 0.0c (Relative Origin).** The relative origin of a framework F is Origin(F) = argmin over D in A(F) of δ(D|F) — the description that minimizes closure deficit within the admissible set. Origin is frame-relative (it depends on F), objectively selected within the frame (δ is a determinate functional), and shiftable under extension (enlarging F may relocate the minimum).

**Theorem 0.0 (Relative-Origin Seed).** *The deepest postulate of the framework is the existence of a relative origin. The binary seed S₀(F) = {0,1} is the minimal selection structure induced by origin-status: 1 = origin-status in F, 0 = non-origin-status relative to F.*

*Proof.* Origin(F) exists as the closure-deficit minimizer within A(F). The indicator of origin-status assigns 1 to the selected origin and 0 to all non-origin descriptions. This induces a minimal two-valued structure {0,1}. The binary seed is therefore not ultimate — it is the minimal formal structure of selection forced by the existence of a relative origin. ∎

**Remark (Relative Origin and K4).** The closure-deficit functional δ(D|F) that selects the relative origin is the same functional that selects the optimal observer in K4 (Paper 5 §11): U_min(K) = argmin δ(U|K). K4 is the observer-level realization of relative origin. The relative origin is not an observer-level construction imported backward — it is the foundational instance of which K4 is the tower lift.

**Remark (No Circularity).** The definitions above require only that a framework triple (L, C, Π) exists. ### §1 TWO CO-PRIMITIVES

Given the existence of a relative origin (§0), the minimal structural content of any framework with Origin(F) defined is two co-primitives — neither separable from the other (Theorem 0.5).

**Postulate P.1 (Recursive Substrate).** A recursive substrate is a domain of transformable structure that supports re-entry: the result of acting within it remains eligible for further action within the same structural field. It requires: (a) persistence under transformation, (b) repeatability, (c) nontrivial internal differentiation potential, (d) orientation-indeterminacy. This is the structural content of a framework's language L: the domain in which descriptions live and compose.

**Postulate P.2 (Productive Distinction).** A productive distinction is a structural condition under which recursive continuation produces sustained, non-exhausting differentiation between states: iteration generates new structure at every step, not merely a finite cycle. This is the structural content of the admissibility filter Π: the condition that selects descriptions capable of sustaining the closure-deficit minimization that defines Origin(F).

Distinction is prior to: observerhood, decomposition, quotienting, and phase orientation.

**Theorem 0.3 (Generative Polarity — Derived).** *Productive distinction is inherently asymmetric. Once recurrence and productive distinction are both present, recursive structure necessarily organizes difference in two contrary directions: folding (concentrates, identifies) and unfolding (releases, separates).* Proof completed in §5 via Theorems 0.11–0.12.

### §1½ THE UNIFIED PRIMITIVE: SELF-RELATING DIFFERENCE

The co-primitives P.1 and P.2 are never found apart (Theorem 0.5). This section identifies their conjunction as a single generative condition and classifies its self-action modes.

**Definition 0.3a (Self-Relating Difference / SRD).** A self-relating difference (SRD) on a domain D is a Dist endomorphism f: (D, ≈) → (D, ≈) with |D| ≥ 2 whose iterates {fⁿ}_{n≥1} are well-defined in the same structural field.

The definition unpacks: *self-relating* = endomorphism (f acts on D and returns to D, so its output is eligible for further action — the content of P.1); *difference* = |D| ≥ 2 with equivalence structure (sustained non-trivial distinction — the content of P.2).

**Theorem 0.3b (Self-Relating Difference Equivalence).** *The following are equivalent:*
*(a) A domain D with operation f satisfies P.1 (recursive substrate) and P.2 (productive distinction) jointly.*
*(b) (D, f) admits self-relating difference: f is a Dist endomorphism on a non-trivial domain.*

*Proof.* (a)→(b): P.1 gives re-entry: the result of f remains in D, so f: D → D is an endomorphism. P.1(c) gives nontrivial internal differentiation, and P.2 gives |D| ≥ 2. The equivalence relation ≈ = ker(f) is forced by the Kernel Theorem (Paper 1 Thm 1.5). (b)→(a): A Dist endomorphism f on |D| ≥ 2 provides persistence (f maps D to D), repeatability (fⁿ is defined for all n), nontrivial differentiation (|D| ≥ 2), and orientation-indeterminacy (f and f⁻¹ are structurally available when f is invertible). This is P.1. The equivalence structure and non-trivial domain give P.2. ∎

### §1½a OBSERVER-RELATIVE EXISTENCE AND THE CONSTITUTIVE BOUNDARY

*(ORE-2) Tower propagation.* The monoidal lift T(n)⊗T(n) (SAFPT-4, MT2) carries constitution to every level: the level-n substrate IS the level-(n−1) fixed point's image. It exists as the output of the level-(n−1) canonical map, not as a free-standing structure. At each level, the domain is constituted by the im/ker decomposition of the canonical observer at that level — the quotient map q at Level 2, the spectral decomposition at Level 3, the observer K at Level 5, the self-classification at Level 7.
