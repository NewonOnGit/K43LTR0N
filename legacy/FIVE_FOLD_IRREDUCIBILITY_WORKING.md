## §4.3 The Folding Commutativity as Independence Engine

**Theorem 2.2 (T3-META).** C∘T = T∘C, where C(M)=M² (within-level composition) and T(M)=M⊗M (cross-level tensor product). The folding algebra is ℤ×ℤ — two independent integer-indexed operations.

This theorem is the operational backbone of the five-fold irreducibility:

- exp is the limit of within-level operations: exp(X) = lim (I+X/n)^n = lim C^n(I+X/n)
- The tower lift is a cross-level operation: T(n) → T(n)⊗T(n) = T(T(n))
- C∘T = T∘C means these two types of lift DO NOT INTERFERE

**Consequence for the five outputs:** The outputs of within-level operations (e, π via exp) and the outputs of cross-level operations (G, Λ via tower-mediated physics; K_meta via tower hierarchy) are produced by commuting operations. Commuting operations generate a product space of outputs. Products of independent mechanisms produce independent outputs (this is exactly the Picard-Vessiot principle that gives 𝔾_m × SO₂ for the within-level (e,π) case).

**The gap:** Picard-Vessiot theory applies to differential equations (within-level). There is no established "Picard-Vessiot theory for monoidal categories" (cross-level). The Folding Commutativity gives operational independence. Converting this to output independence requires a theorem of the form: "commuting operations on the tower produce algebraically/structurally independent outputs." This is the precise mathematical target.
