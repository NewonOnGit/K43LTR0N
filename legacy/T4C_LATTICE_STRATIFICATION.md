## Abstract

The Λ' lattice has five coordinate directions {φ, e, π, √2, √3}. An empirical pattern in lattice-approximated physical constants shows that each constant is dominated by a specific generator: lepton and electroweak mass ratios by φ, decay rates by e, confinement ratios by π, three-body binding structures by √3, and normalization/interference amplitudes by √2. This paper proves the pattern is structural: a physical constant's dominant lattice coordinate is determined by the GL(2,ℝ) orbit type of the dynamical process that generates it.

We define the projection type of a physical constant precisely, prove the five classification theorems (φ-dominance for det < 0 processes, e-dominance for hyperbolic/Δ > 0 processes, π-dominance for elliptic/Δ < 0 processes, √3-dominance for S₃-representation-level non-compact processes, √2-dominance for norm-preserving compact processes), and resolve the π paradox: π is the most mathematically necessary constant (forcing quality: absolute) yet the least frequent in measured mass spectra because confinement (the physical process governed by P3) produces ratios rather than absolute masses.

## §2 FIVE CLASSIFICATION THEOREMS

**Theorem C1 (φ-Dominance).** *Physical constants generated by orientation-reversing processes have φ as dominant lattice coordinate.* Mechanism: det(R) = −1, eigenvalue ratio φ/φ̄ = φ² governs the suppression.

**Theorem C2 (e-Dominance).** *Physical constants generated by hyperbolic processes have e as dominant lattice coordinate.* Mechanism: exp(t·h) = diag(eᵗ, e⁻ᵗ) governs exponential growth/decay.

**Theorem C3 (π-Dominance).** *Physical constants generated by elliptic processes have π as dominant lattice coordinate.* Mechanism: exp(θN) rotates; the half-period π governs periodic structure.

**Theorem C4 (√3-Dominance).** *Physical constants arising from S₃ representation structure have √3 as dominant coordinate.* Mechanism: √3 = ||R||_F = 2sin(2π/3) is the S₃ equilateral structure invariant.

**Theorem C5 (√2-Dominance).** *Physical constants generated by norm-preserving (compact) processes have √2 as dominant lattice coordinate.* Mechanism: √2 = ||N||_F is the Frobenius norm of the rotation generator. Where π governs the period of rotation (spectral data: exp(πN) = −I), √2 governs the amplitude — the geometric scale of the compact group action.

The two geometric constants form a generator pair mirroring {R, N}:

| Norm constant | Generator | Orbit type | Process type | Physical domain |
|---------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| √3 = ||R||_F | R (P1) | Non-compact | Angular, equilateral | Three-body binding, S₃ representation |
| √2 = ||N||_F | N (P3) | Compact | Rotational amplitude | Normalization, interference, spin amplitudes |

Their ratio √3/√2 = √(3/2) = √(1/Q_Koide) connects the two norm directions through the Koide constraint.

## §3 THE π PARADOX

π has the highest forcing quality (absolute — zero ambiguity) yet appears least frequently as the dominant coordinate in measured mass spectra. Resolution: the physical processes governed by P3 (confinement, periodicity) produce **dimensionless ratios** (e.g., circumference/diameter, period/amplitude), not absolute mass values. Mass spectra are dominated by P1 (φ) because orientation-reversal (symmetry breaking, mass generation) is the process that **creates** mass differences. π governs the *structure* of confinement, not the *scale* of masses.

## §4 DEEP LATTICE CONNECTIONS (FROM LDI)

The lattice stratification connects to the broader framework through several unifications found in the deep investigation:

**Orbit type determines not just which coordinate dominates, but the functional form.** φ-dominant constants appear as powers φ^n or ratios φ^{n}/φ^{m}. e-dominant constants appear as exp(±t) or exp(−Γt). π-dominant constants appear as multiples of π or as arguments of trigonometric functions. √3-dominant constants appear as √3 or 2cos(2πk/3).

**The Killing metric on the (r,d,c) sublattice** (Paper 4A §3, Layer 5) has signature (2,1) with φ and e Killing-coupled (both in the positive cone) and π Killing-orthogonal (in the negative cone). This reproduces the Two-World Separation at the lattice level: the e-world and π-world are geometrically separated by the Killing light cone.

**The Killing cone acquires a transcendence-theoretic interpretation through the Sector Rigidity program (Paper 4A §8.1–8.9).** The lattice coordinates (r, d, c) decompose into Killing-sign sectors:

- **Positive Killing cone** (B > 0): coordinates (r, d, 0) — constants dominated by φ and e. The algebraic φ and transcendental e are Killing-coupled; their independence follows from Lindemann-Weierstrass (e transcendental, φ algebraic).
- **Negative Killing cone** (B < 0): coordinates (0, 0, c) — constants dominated by π. The transcendental π has B < 0 source; its independence from φ follows from Lindemann-Weierstrass.
- **The (d, c) plane:** the joint transcendental coordinates. The (e,π) independence question IS the question of whether the positive and negative Killing cones are algebraically disjoint in the transcendental sublattice.
- **Killing boundary** (B = 0): the nilpotent cone. Produces only algebraic exponential output (Paper 2A, Theorem 5.6) — a transcendence desert carrying zero transcendental lattice content.

The two norm generators √2 and √3 are *pre-Killing*: they arise from Frobenius norms, not from the exponential map of sl(2,ℝ), and sit outside the Killing-cone structure entirely. Their independence from each other and from the spectral constants is unconditional (Baker's theorem, Lindemann-Weierstrass).

The period wall (Paper 2A, Theorem 5.8) shows that the Killing boundary is not merely a formal separator: approaching it from the elliptic (π) side, the half-period diverges to infinity while the exponential output collapses to the algebraic value 3/2. The boundary is a period wall and a transcendence desert simultaneously — impenetrable to polynomial relations.
